03 — Journal

Moments I carried with me

Pohela Boishakh · Cultural
Standing on a map of a land I love

There's something quietly profound about celebrating Pohela Boishakh — the Bengali New Year — dressed in tradition, camera in hand, surrounded by colour and rhythm. This image was crafted as a tribute to Bangladesh: the Sundarbans, Cox's Bazar, the rolling hills of Bandarban, and the grandeur of the National Parliament. I am from this land, and every journey I take starts from its soil.

Culture · Festival
Lost in a Boishakhi fair

White panjabi, the scent of earthen pots, and the sound of drums in the distance. Pohela Boishakh fairs have a way of slowing time down — I wandered through stalls of handcrafted goods, paused to watch, and let the colours wash over me.

Life · Candid
A quiet Tuesday that felt like a movie

Black-and-white floors, metal chairs, low light. Some days don't ask for a reason — they just happen beautifully. I sat there, unbothered, watching the world move around me at its own pace.
